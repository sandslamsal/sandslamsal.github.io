Sandesh Lamsal

Civil/Structural Engineer. Florida, USA.

1251 Memorial Drive, McArthur Eng. Bldg

Coral Gables, FL 33146

Structural Engineer, researcher, and Ph.D. student in Civil and Architectural Engineering at the University of Miami, Ph.D. Candidate 2025. His academic journey began with a Bachelor’s Degree in Civil Engineering from Tribhuvan University, followed by a Master of Science in Civil Engineering from Texas Tech University. Prior to his enrollment at UMiami, he also honed his skills as a Civil Engineer in Nepal

Currently engaged in morphological investigation and physical testing of Submerged Structures at the University of Miami’s Alfred C. Glassell, Jr. Surge-Structure-Atmosphere Interaction (SUSTAIN) Laboratory. This exciting research is conducted under the guidance of Dr. Landolf Rhode-Barbarigos (CASE Lab). He is also involved in studying the combination of wind and wave loading effects in building structures and considerations for prevailing building design codes within his team.

His professional and academic journey is a diverse one, stretching from the design of structures capable of withstanding earthquakes to his present emphasis on enhancing coastal resilience. He thrives on delving into a variety of research challenges, always with the aim of making significant and impactful contributions to the field of engineering. Beyond his research, he is a vibrant part of the University of Miami Graduate community, actively participating in the American Society of Civil Engineers Student Society, and contributing to the Graduate Student Association.
